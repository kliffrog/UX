About

The purpose of this website is to present a brief overview of the extensive subject of user experience (UX).

Author

Keith is a student at JCCC majoring in computer science. He hopes to obtain the knowledge needed to succeed in the computer information industry.

UX Methodologies

There are three main methods researchers use to obtain user information: observation, understanding, and analysis.

Observation:

Observation is simply observing the world around us. Take notes on how, when and where customers use your product. Look for patterns or similarities.

Understanding:

Everyone has different backgrounds and upbringings. Some are educated. Some have common sense. We all have a mental picture of how things should be. Being able to see past someone's mental model and accommodate for that is a key ingredient to UX.

Analysis:

Studying, collaborating and sharing research findings are all part of the analysis process. Research can only be used if it is available to your designers.
